“BLUE MARBLE”


The EPIC provides information on the daily imagery collected by DSCOVR's Earth Polychromatic Imaging Camera (EPIC) instrument. Uniquely positioned at the Earth-Sun Lagrange point, EPIC provides full disc imagery of the Earth and captures unique perspectives of certain astronomical events such as lunar transits using a 2048x2048 pixel CCD (Charge Coupled Device) detector coupled to a 30-cm aperture Cassegrain telescope. Development of the EPIC began in 2015, and is supported by the web development team for the Laboratory for Atmospheres in the Earth Sciences Division of the Goddard Space Flight Center.
